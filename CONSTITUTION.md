# FounderOS Constitution

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Accepted

## Purpose

The FounderOS Constitution defines the enduring principles that govern every
project, engineering kit, AI agent, workflow, and publication created within
the FounderOS ecosystem.

It should change rarely. All other documents, specifications, and
implementations must align with this constitution.

## Article I — Mission

FounderOS exists to help founders repeatedly transform ideas into successful
companies through structured knowledge, disciplined execution, and
AI-assisted automation.

## Article II — Foundational Principles

1. Documentation before implementation.
2. Research before commitment.
3. Solve real founder problems.
4. Prefer small, verifiable milestones over large speculative efforts.
5. Human judgment remains accountable for strategic decisions.
6. Automation must be reproducible and observable.
7. Open standards are preferred over vendor lock-in.
8. Knowledge compounds through reuse.

## Article III — Canonical Sources

The authoritative sources of project knowledge are:

- The Git repository
- Markdown documents
- Version-controlled specifications
- Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)

Derived formats—including Notion pages, websites, presentations, PDF, EPUB,
and DOCX files—are publishing outputs, not sources of truth.

The root `CONSTITUTION.md` is the only canonical FounderOS constitution.

## Article IV — Engineering Standards

Every project must include, where appropriate:

- Vision
- Worthiness review
- Product requirements document
- Architecture Decision Records
- Milestone plan
- Acceptance criteria
- Verification checklist
- Change log

No implementation should bypass these foundations without an explicit,
documented justification.

## Article V — AI Governance

AI agents may assist with research, drafting, coding, testing, documentation,
and publishing.

AI agents must:

- Identify assumptions
- Distinguish synthesis from sourced facts
- Preserve traceability
- Avoid inventing unsupported claims
- Produce reproducible outputs

Strategic decisions require human approval.

## Article VI — Quality Gates

Before publication or release:

- Documentation has been reviewed.
- Acceptance criteria are satisfied.
- References are validated.
- Diagrams are verified.
- Required automation passes.
- The version is updated where applicable.
- The change log is updated.

## Article VII — Knowledge Stewardship

Research, lessons learned, templates, prompts, and reusable assets should be
preserved for future projects.

FounderOS is designed to compound value over time rather than optimize for
one-off execution.

## Article VIII — Continuous Improvement

Projects should include retrospectives. Improvements discovered in one project
should be evaluated for adoption across the FounderOS ecosystem.

## Article IX — Governance

Changes to this constitution must be rare, deliberate, documented, and
accompanied by an Architecture Decision Record explaining the rationale.

Major changes to architecture, source-of-truth policy, public licensing, or
core platform commitments require human approval.

## Guiding Statement

Every project built with FounderOS should leave the ecosystem stronger than it
was before.
