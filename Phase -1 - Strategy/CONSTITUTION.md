# FounderOS Constitution

Version: 1.0 (Foundational Draft)

## Purpose

The FounderOS Constitution defines the enduring principles that govern
every project, engineering kit, AI agent, workflow, and publication
created within the FounderOS ecosystem.

It is intended to change rarely. All other documents, specifications,
and implementations should align with this Constitution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article I --- Mission

FounderOS exists to help founders repeatedly transform ideas into
successful companies through structured knowledge, disciplined
execution, and AI-assisted automation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article II --- Foundational Principles

1.  Documentation before implementation.
2.  Research before commitment.
3.  Solve real founder problems.
4.  Small, verifiable milestones over large speculative efforts.
5.  Human judgment remains accountable for strategic decisions.
6.  Automation must be reproducible and observable.
7.  Open standards are preferred over vendor lock-in.
8.  Knowledge compounds through reuse.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article III --- Canonical Sources

The authoritative source of project knowledge shall be:

-   Git repository
-   Markdown documents
-   Version-controlled specifications
-   Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)

Derived formats (PDF, Notion, websites, presentations) are publishing
outputs, not the source of truth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article IV --- Engineering Standards

Every project shall include, where appropriate:

-   Vision
-   Worthiness Review
-   Product Requirements Document
-   Architecture Decision Records
-   Milestone plan
-   Acceptance criteria
-   Verification checklist
-   Change log

No implementation should bypass these foundations without explicit
justification.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article V --- AI Governance

AI agents may assist with research, drafting, coding, testing,
documentation, and publishing.

AI agents must:

-   identify assumptions
-   distinguish synthesis from sourced facts
-   preserve traceability
-   avoid inventing unsupported claims
-   produce reproducible outputs

Strategic decisions require human approval.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article VI --- Quality Gates

Before publication or release:

-   Documentation reviewed
-   Acceptance criteria satisfied
-   References validated
-   Diagrams verified
-   Automation passes
-   Version updated
-   Change log completed

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article VII --- Knowledge Stewardship

Research, lessons learned, templates, prompts, and reusable assets
should be preserved for future projects.

FounderOS is designed to compound value over time rather than optimize
for one-off execution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article VIII --- Continuous Improvement

Projects should include retrospectives.

Improvements discovered in one project should be evaluated for adoption
across the FounderOS ecosystem.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article IX --- Governance

Changes to this Constitution should be rare, deliberate, documented, and
accompanied by an Architecture Decision Record explaining the rationale.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Guiding Statement

Every project built with FounderOS should leave the ecosystem stronger
than it was before.
