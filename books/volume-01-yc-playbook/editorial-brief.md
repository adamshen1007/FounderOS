# Editorial Brief — The FounderOS Playbook for AI Founders

## Canonical Reader

The primary reader is a first-time founder who has an AI-enabled product idea
but has not yet established repeatable user value. The reader may be technical,
nontechnical, solo, or part of a small founding team. They need an ordered way
to decide what to learn, build, measure, and stop doing.

The secondary reader is an early team member or advisor helping that founder
turn ambiguous discussion into a concrete next action.

## Editorial Promise

After using this playbook, the reader should be able to:

- describe a specific user, painful situation, and proposed outcome;
- separate assumptions from observed evidence;
- plan and launch a small, useful product test;
- evaluate behavior, retention, AI quality, cost, and risk;
- choose a weekly priority without surrendering judgment to an AI tool;
- make explicit continue, change, pause, or stop decisions; and
- maintain a simple operating record for the first 90 days.

The book promises a decision system, not a success formula.

## Positioning and Independence

This is an independent FounderOS publication that synthesizes public startup
teaching from Y Combinator and other identified primary sources. FounderOS is
not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Y Combinator. The public
title uses the FounderOS name; every attributed claim about Y Combinator must
remain traceable to its source.

The book must distinguish:

- **Sourced guidance:** a claim attributed to an identified publication;
- **FounderOS synthesis:** a practical connection across two or more ideas; and
- **Assumption:** something the founder must test in their own context.

## Scope

The book covers the journey from founder readiness through the first 90 days:

- problem choice and assumption mapping;
- user discovery and market focus;
- minimum useful product design and launch;
- AI reliability, cost, safety, and human accountability;
- first customers, behavior, retention, and prioritization;
- weekly cadence, runway, team decisions, and fundraising choice; and
- a reusable founder operating system.

## Deliberate Exclusions

The book does not provide:

- legal, tax, accounting, immigration, medical, or investment advice;
- a directory of current AI models, vendors, prices, or regulations;
- instructions for gaming a YC application or imitating a YC company;
- a guarantee of product-market fit, venture funding, or rapid growth;
- a substitute for direct user contact or qualified professional review; or
- an autonomous AI agent that makes consequential founder decisions.

## Editorial Principles

1. Begin with the founder decision, then explain the concept.
2. Prefer observed behavior over compliments and forecasts.
3. Use plain language and define unavoidable terms on first use.
4. Keep examples specific without inventing real company facts.
5. Give AI a bounded role: assist execution, surface options, preserve evidence.
6. State uncertainty, tradeoffs, and stopping conditions.
7. End every chapter with an action, worksheet, and review point.
8. Cite primary or first-party sources for external claims.

## Voice and Reading Level

Write directly to “you” in calm, practical language. Avoid investor theater,
hero-founder mythology, and unexplained acronyms. A beginner should understand
each chapter without reading external material, while an experienced reader
should still find the decision tools precise.

## Chapter Contract

Every chapter must contain the elements required by `AGENTS.md`: learning
objectives, a core principle, deep-dive explanation, AI founder interpretation,
callout boxes, a diagram when it materially improves understanding, a checklist,
a worksheet, key takeaways, and sources for external claims.

## Editorial Acceptance Criteria

The first complete manuscript is acceptable for internal review when:

- all planned chapters exist in the canonical order;
- every chapter passes the automated chapter contract;
- claims and quotations pass citation and copyright checks;
- the manuscript builds to HTML, EPUB, and DOCX;
- no chapter implies YC endorsement or guaranteed outcomes;
- cross-chapter terminology and decision states are consistent; and
- any beta-reader or pilot claims are supported by actual records.

Publication remains a human decision after editorial and legal review.
