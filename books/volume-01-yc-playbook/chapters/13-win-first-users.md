# Chapter 13 — Win Your First Users

> **Core Principle:** Recruit early users personally and make their first useful
> outcome unusually easy to reach.

## Learning Objectives

- Choose one direct acquisition motion for the focused wedge.
- Write outreach around a real problem instead of a product announcement.
- Combine acquisition, onboarding, service, and learning.

## Deep Dive

Your first users rarely arrive because a launch is loud. They arrive because a
founder identifies a relevant person, understands the situation, and helps that
person reach an outcome.

YC’s essential advice tells early startups to get initial customers by direct,
even manual means.[^essential] Paul Graham likewise recommends recruiting users
manually and giving them exceptional attention.[^scale] This work is not beneath
the product; it reveals what the product must become.

Choose one source of relevant people: prior colleagues, a professional group,
a local workflow, targeted outbound, or a community where you already
contribute. Write a short message naming the observed problem, why the person
may be relevant, and the small test you are offering. Do not claim proof or
scale you do not have.

Onboard personally. Observe where trust, data, setup, or explanation slows the
first useful outcome. Record those obstacles before automating them. The goal is
not a vanity signup count; it is a small group whose behavior teaches you.

## AI Founder Interpretation

AI can research public context, draft variants, and organize follow-up. It must
not impersonate personal knowledge, mass-send unreviewed messages, or use
private data without a valid basis.

Keep outreach human, specific, and easy to decline. Review generated claims for
truth before sending.

## Callouts

### Decision Lens

> **Decision Lens:** Where can you find ten people who recently experienced the
> exact situation in your problem statement?

### Common Failure

> **Common Failure:** Optimizing a public launch before learning how to help one
> relevant user succeed.

## Checklist

- [ ] Choose one reachable source of relevant early users.
- [ ] Write a truthful problem-led outreach message.
- [ ] Invite a small test with a clear next step.
- [ ] Onboard personally and observe friction.
- [ ] Record outcome, objection, and follow-up for each contact.

## Key Takeaways

- The first acquisition motion should maximize relevant learning.
- Manual onboarding can expose the product’s real requirements.
- Registrations matter less than completed useful outcomes.
- AI may assist outreach but cannot justify false familiarity or mass spam.
