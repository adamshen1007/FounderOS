# Chapter 5 — Talk to Users

> **Core Principle:** Ask about specific past behavior before asking for opinions
> about your proposed future.

## Learning Objectives

- Recruit people connected to the problem you are testing.
- Ask questions that reveal situations, actions, alternatives, and consequences.
- Convert notes into evidence without hiding contradictions.

## Deep Dive

A useful user conversation is not a sales pitch with a question mark at the end.
Your goal is to reconstruct what happened: the trigger, the work, the current
alternative, the people involved, and the consequence.

YC’s Startup School includes user conversations as a core early-founder topic,
and YC’s user-observation guidance frames early design as a way to understand
users.[^school][^observation] FounderOS turns that guidance into a simple rule:
collect stories before forecasts.

Ask, “Tell me about the last time this happened.” Follow with “What did you do
next?”, “Who else was involved?”, and “What made that difficult?” Avoid leading
questions such as “Would an AI assistant save you time?” The latter introduces
your solution and invites politeness.

Recruit for relevance, not convenience. Five people with the same title may
have different workflows. Record why each participant is informative. After the
conversation, separate direct observation, participant report, and your
interpretation. A contradiction is valuable because it narrows the problem.

## AI Founder Interpretation

AI can draft a neutral interview guide, de-identify notes, and group recurring
events. It must not invent missing quotes or flatten minority cases into a false
consensus. Review every summary against the original notes.

Never record or upload a conversation without appropriate permission. Minimize
personal and confidential data before using any AI service.

## Callouts

### Decision Lens

> **Decision Lens:** What recent behavior would make this person informative
> about the assumption you ranked first?

### Common Failure

> **Common Failure:** Counting enthusiasm as demand. “That sounds useful” is a
> social response; a costly workaround is stronger evidence of a real problem.

## Checklist

- [ ] Name the assumption this conversation will inform.
- [ ] Recruit participants based on relevant recent behavior.
- [ ] Ask about the last specific occurrence before presenting a solution.
- [ ] Record alternatives, consequences, and contradictions.
- [ ] Remove sensitive details and review summaries against notes.

## Key Takeaways

- Past behavior is usually more useful than hypothetical approval.
- Participant relevance matters more than interview count alone.
- Contradictions improve the model when they remain visible.
- AI may organize notes, but the founder owns consent and interpretation.
