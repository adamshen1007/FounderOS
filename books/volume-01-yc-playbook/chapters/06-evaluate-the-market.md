# Chapter 6 — Evaluate the Market

> **Core Principle:** Estimate a market from reachable customers and delivered
> value, not from a large industry number.

## Learning Objectives

- Distinguish a broad market story from a useful starting estimate.
- Build a bottom-up model from customer count, frequency, and value.
- Decide whether the opportunity can support your actual company goal.

## Deep Dive

A market estimate is a decision aid, not a prediction. At this stage you need to
know whether a focused starting group is reachable, experiences the problem,
and could exchange enough value to support your goal.

YC’s Startup Playbook treats market and growth as parts of the company system,
while Paul Graham defines a startup in relation to growth.[^playbook][^growth]
Those perspectives do not justify multiplying an industry report by an
arbitrary percentage.

Build from the bottom up:

1. Define the narrow customer profile and buying unit.
2. Estimate how many such units you can identify in one starting geography or
   channel.
3. Estimate annual value from observed cost, frequency, or budget—not a desired
   price.
4. Apply explicit reach, conversion, retention, and capacity assumptions.
5. calculate a low, base, and high case.

The result may show a good small business, a credible venture path, or an
insufficient opportunity. None is morally superior. Compare the result with the
company you want to build and the resources the path requires.

## AI Founder Interpretation

AI can generate market-model scenarios and expose arithmetic errors. It can
also produce precise-looking customer counts without evidence. Require a source
or an “assumption” label for every input.

Keep the model simple enough to explain without AI. Update it when interviews,
sales, or usage contradict an input.

## Callouts

### Decision Lens

> **Decision Lens:** If you served only the reachable starting segment, would
> the outcome justify the required years, capital, and risk?

### Common Failure

> **Common Failure:** Calling one percent of a giant market conservative. The
> percentage says nothing about access, competition, capacity, or willingness
> to change.

## Checklist

- [ ] Define the customer and buying unit precisely.
- [ ] List the source or assumption behind every input.
- [ ] Model low, base, and high cases.
- [ ] Include reach, conversion, retention, price, and delivery capacity.
- [ ] Compare the result with the founder’s desired company type.

## Key Takeaways

- Bottom-up estimates reveal the assumptions a founder can test.
- A large category does not prove a reachable market.
- The right market depends on the company outcome and resource path you choose.
- AI can calculate scenarios but should not silently invent inputs.
