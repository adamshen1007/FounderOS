# Chapter 16 — Decide What to Build Next

> **Core Principle:** Prioritize the change that addresses the strongest user
> evidence and the most important remaining uncertainty.

## Learning Objectives

- Turn requests, failures, and opportunities into comparable decision items.
- Rank work by user consequence, evidence, learning value, and effort.
- Commit to one product priority with a review rule.

## Deep Dive

Early users will request features, and founders will see technical improvements,
sales promises, and AI possibilities. A backlog captures ideas; it does not
decide among them.

YC’s essential advice recommends focus and a high-leverage approach to product
work.[^essential] Michael Seibel’s product-market-fit guidance warns against
assuming the product is working before demand is unmistakable.[^fit] Therefore,
prioritize work that strengthens the focused value loop or tests why it fails.

For each item, record the user and situation, observed evidence, consequence of
inaction, assumption tested, expected decision value, effort, and reversibility.
Rank evidence before confidence. A founder idea may be valuable, but label it as
an assumption rather than presenting it as a user request.

Choose one primary item for the next loop. Write what you are not doing and why.
This protects the team from silently reopening the decision each day.

## AI Founder Interpretation

AI can cluster feedback and draft ranking tables. It may overcount repeated
wording from the same source or treat vocal users as representative. Preserve
source IDs and review the original context.

Never let an AI-generated score hide the judgment behind the weights.

## Callouts

### Decision Lens

> **Decision Lens:** Which change would most improve the value loop or clarify
> why users fail to complete it?

### Common Failure

> **Common Failure:** Building every request from an early customer and turning
> a focused product into custom consulting software.

## Checklist

- [ ] Attach every item to a user, situation, and evidence source.
- [ ] Record consequence, learning value, effort, and reversibility.
- [ ] Separate requests, observations, and founder assumptions.
- [ ] Choose one primary item for the next loop.
- [ ] Publish a not-now list and review date.

## Key Takeaways

- A backlog records possibilities; a decision system sets focus.
- Early product work should strengthen or explain the core value loop.
- Source context prevents loud feedback from becoming false consensus.
- A clear not-now list is part of a real priority decision.
